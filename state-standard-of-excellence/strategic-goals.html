2019 State Standard of Excellence

Strategic Goals

Back to
Standard

Did the Governor have public statewide strategic goals?

Why is this important?
The creation of statewide strategic goals is an important first step in aligning goals, expenditures, and programmatic activities as a way to improve outcomes on a state government’s highest priorities.

Leading Example

CO

Statewide

Issue 1

The Colorado Governor’s Office dashboard outlines four high-priority strategic goals: tax reform and economic development; energy and renewables; health; and education and the workforce. Each goal is aligned with a subset of measurable metrics and goals and the strategies for accomplishing them.

Promising Examples

MN

Single agency

Issue 3
Issue 4

The Minnesota Management and Budget (MMB) led a process which required state agencies to document the evidence of effectiveness for proposals for new programs as well as proposals to change or expand existing activities. The Governor’s Office used this information, combined with input from communities across the state, and included 17 proposals ($150 million) with strong causal evidence of effectiveness in the FY 2020-2021 Governor’s Budget. The enacted budget ultimately included $87 million in evidence-based proposals, including items initiated by the legislature.

NC

Statewide

Issue 2

North Carolina’s Office of State Budget and Management (OSBM) strategic planning initiative, Systems Analysis, combines of strategic planning, operational improvement analysis, and ongoing performance monitoring with the goal of evaluating statewide and agency-specific operations, identifying areas of progress, and developing, executing, and tracking plans for the future. OSBM provides resources, such as a memo, guidance, and strategic plan template, for developing strategic plans that align the governor’s priority goals. The strategic plans are publicly available.

OR

Statewide

Issue 2

The 2018 Action Plan for Oregon identified key priorities with aligned measures, strategies, and plans in the areas of: environment, healthcare, education, transportation, good government, earthquake preparedness, housing and homelessness, child welfare, and job training.

UT

Statewide

Issue 1

The Utah Governor’s Office of Management and Budget’s SUCCESS Framework includes three phases as part of the Utah Life Elevated 2020 Initiative: effective and efficient government, thriving communities, qualified workforce, and equitable and competitive revenue. SUCCESS Framework resources assist agencies to reach their performance goals. One such agency goal focused on reducing opioid overdoses which resulted in 13% decline in opioid use.
