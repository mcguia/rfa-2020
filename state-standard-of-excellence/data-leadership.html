2019 State Standard of Excellence

Data Leadership

Back to
Standard

Did the governor’s office or any state agency have a senior staff member(s) with the authority, staff, and budget to collect, analyze, share, and use high-quality administrative and survey data—consistent with strong privacy protections—to improve (or help other entities including but not limited to local governments and nonprofit organizations improve) federal, state, and local programs? (Example: chief data officer)

Why is this important?
A designated chief data officer can create a coherent set of policies, structures, and guidance for how a state government and its agencies should routinely use data to improve programs.

Leading Example

IN

Statewide

Issue 4
Issue 5

A 2017 Indiana law established the position of chief data officer (p. 8) with the budget, staff, and authority to (1) coordinate data analytics and data transparency for state agencies; (2) advise state agencies regarding best practices for data maintenance, security, and privacy; and (3) oversee the Indiana Management Performance Hub, which uses state data to provide “analytics solutions tailored to address complex management and policy questions enabling improved outcomes.” This 2018 Annual Report estimates a return on investment of $40 million for the State.

Promising Examples

KY

Statewide

Issue 2

In 2018, Kentucky established a Chief Data Officer as the chief data steward to “coordinate and oversee the sharing of data and shall implement effective data governance strategies designed to maintain data integrity, confidentiality, availability, security, and to promote access to data.” The accompanying Enterprise Data Management Policy established the Office of Data, Information, and Analytics (ODIA), led by the CDO, and required Executive Branch agencies to identify an Agency Data Steward that would report directly to the Cabinet Secretary or Agency Head. The Data Steward would carry out the functions of the data stewardship across the state through the master data sharing agreement, acting as liaison to the CDO/ODIA.

OR

Statewide

Issue 2

A 2018 Oregon law created a state Chief Data Officer who is responsible for centralized data strategy, including an enterprise wide open data platform. The Chief Data Officer is responsible for creating open data standards; for providing information protection and privacy guidance for state agencies; identify opportunities for data sharing to support state efforts internally and externally with partners; and design stakeholder engagement strategies including an advisory committee and public engagement component.

UT

Statewide

Issue 2

Utah’s Chief Data Officer, a member of the Operational Excellence team, uses QT/OE to help agencies measure what matters, including an effort to merge social services case management data.
